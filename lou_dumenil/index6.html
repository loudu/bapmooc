Books and articles to learn more about computers

The Universal History of Computing : From the Abacus to the Quantum Computer

Georges Ifrah, John Wiley & Sons, New York, 2001.

This book traces the development of computing from the invention of the abacus to the creation of the binary system three centuries ago to the incredible conceptual, scientific, and technical achievements that made the first modern computers possible. 

 

First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC

John von Neumann, Contract n. W-670-ORD-4926, Moore School of Electrical Engineering, University of Pennsylvania, 1945.

 The original typescript draft by John von Neumann at the origin of the stored-program architecture used in modern computers. A corrected copy is available on the web by Michael D. Godfrey, Stanford University. 

Mechanical Intelligence - Collected Works of A. M. Turing

 Darrel C. Ince (editor), North-Holland, Elsevier Science Publishers, Amsterdam, 1992.

The collected papers of Alan Turing, the english pioneer researcher in computer architecture, software engineering and artificial intelligence.

  .

Computer Architecture - A Quantitative Approach 

    John L. Hennessy and David A. Patterson, Morgan Kaufmann Publishers, Palo Alto, 1990.

The computer architecture course at Berkeley and Stanford from the two inventors of the RISC approach. Recommended for people with some experience in digital design. 

RISC Architectures

   Jean-Claude Heudin and Christian Panetto, Chapman and Hall, London, 1992.

 This book covers the principles and techniques that make RISC a unique and elegant approach for high-performance microprocessor system design.

 

From transistor to Computer - An introduction to the world of Computer Architecture 

   Claude Timsit and Soraya Zertal, Hermann, Paris, 2013.

This book proposes an intuitive method to understand computer architecture with simple tools using spreadsheets, starting with transistors, logical gates, up to microprocessors.

The Art of Assembly Language

  Randall Hyde, No Starch Press, San Francisco, 2010.

This book provides a comprehensive and patient introduction to assembly language, to help you quickly grasp basic assembly programming concepts.
